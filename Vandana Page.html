Patient Rights & Responsibilities

PATIENT RIGHTS

CARE & TREATMENT

As a patient you must be aware of the use of research or experimental treatments in your care. You have the choice to agree or decline the treatment.

SUPPORTIVE CARE

You can have a good supportive care including pain management, treating of uncomfortable symptoms and providing support for your psychological and spiritual needs.

ACCEPT OR REFUSE TREATMENT

To agree or reject any procedure, medication, or treatment, and to be aware of the implications of any such rejection. You and your parents or guardian may need to take part in a dispute resolution procedure if there is a disagreement about how you should use this privilege.

RESPECTFUL TREATMENT

Personal dignity has to be respected and should be treated with courtesy by the hospital management.

COMPLAINT & FEEDBACK

To express concerns about your care, have those concerns looked into and if feasible, have those concerns provide remedies without fear of punishment or harm to yourself. You have the right to know how your compliant was addressed.

PERSONAL PRIVACY

To maintain absolute discretion during all care-related conversations, consultations, physicals and treatments. Discussions about care, exams and treatments will be kept highly confidential.

CONFIDENTIALITY

To anticipate that all the conversations and documents pertaining to your care will be kept private.

TRANSFER TO ANOTHER FACILITY

To be moved to a different facility at your choice or when it’s both medically and legally appropriate to do so. You have the right to get a thorough justification for the purpose of transfer and the receiving unit should accept you as patient for the process to be happened.

TREATMENT

To receive treatment without regard to one’s mental abilities, ethnicity, religion or disability.

SECOND ADVICE

To help you get a second opinion from a doctor about your treatment. You or your family may incur additional charges for this consultation.

MEDICAL REPORT

To have your primary consultant grant you access to the data of your medical file.

CARING TEAM

To know the complete details of all the personnel’s responsible for your treatment and mention in detail about the physician primarily responsible for your care.

DIAGNOSIS, TREATMENT & PROGNOSIS

To be given accurate information in plain language about your diagnosis, course of treatment, and prognosis.

BILL EXPLANATION

Regardless of the source of funding for your care, the patient has the right to see the complete breakdown of the bill charges.

PROCEDURE EXPLANATION

Have a clear explanation of any recommended procedures, medication, or treatment, including the potential advantages, and known major side effects, risks, prospective expenses, issues with recovery, and the possibility of success. Explanation of alternative methods or therapies should be presented.

PATIENT RESPONSIBILITIES

HEALTH INFORMATION

The patients should make sure to share their all-general information related to their previous illness, family history which helps in providing better care. This includes reporting if you are in pain or have taken a pain relief.

TRANSFER OF CARE

If you want to move from one doctor, care provider, or facility to another, you should let your doctor or other care provider know the necessity.

ASK QUESTIONS

When any instructions mentioned by health care provider is not understandable the patient has to make sure to clarify them without making any delay.

PARTICIPATION

To make the decision that is best possible to your ability regarding your medical treatment and agree with the plan of care provided by the health care provider.

RESPECT OTHERS

To show consideration for those giving and receiving care.

COMPLIANCE

To strictly follow all the policies provided by the facility regarding smoking, number of visitors and noise.

PAY FEES

To settle all the bills promptly according to the due date without fail and patient is completely responsible regarding this.

VIOLENCE FREE

Respect all the staff providing care without any type of abuse, violence or inappropriate behavior. Any violations to these will not be tolerated.
